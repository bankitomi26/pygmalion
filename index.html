How the subtitle relates with my second picture?

Under Suprematism I understand the primacy of pure feeling in creative art. To the Suprematist, the visual phenomena of the objective world are, in themselves, meaningless; the significant thing is feeling, as such, quite apart from the environment in which it is called forth.” We have seen this type of approach to art ones before in history, that with Idealism, which emerged during the Renaissance. Idealism regarded the mind and the spirit to be superior to the physical body and world. It chooses the psyche over the material and the historical. Idealist painters such as Leonardo, Raphael and Michelangelo, all interpreted Idealism in their own way, and while Michelangelo’s works idealise in a way that show the artist’s imagination rather than imitation, hence his monumentally muscular subjects, Raphael depicted his figures in a rather sweet and elegant light, pointing out the divinity in the perfectly human form. While at first glance the works of Michelangelo and Malevich may seem very different, the similarities lie in the underlying psychology of both artists, in the sense that they are both concerned with the metaphysical and emotional as opposed to the tangible.

Malevich’s Suprematism does not believe in the humanist philosophy, which places the human being at the center, however, it does envision man as the originator and the transmitter in the world which the artist envisions to be completely non-objective. For Malevich the future of the world will be built free of objects, appearances, comfort and conveniences, and will be reliant purely on the the psychological and metaphysical.
